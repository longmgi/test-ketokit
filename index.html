The Dangers of Insulin

Whenever you eat, your pancreas releases a hormone, called insulin.

The role of insulin in the body is to regulate blood sugar levels by allowing cells to absorb sugar for energy.

However, when insulin is present in the bloodstream, the body CANNOT burn fat. Plus, the more insulin released into the body (especially with high carb diets), the less effective it is at moving glucose out of the blood, leading to insulin resistance.

If left untreated, this can lead to Type 2 Diabetes and the other conditions I mentioned above.

The Healthy Keto Lifestyle with Intermittent Fasting

Unlike other weight loss programs, Healthy Keto and Intermittent Fasting involves:

  • Cutting out carbohydrates completely
  • Fasting for intermittent periods throughout the week
  • Putting your body in the new metabolic state of ketosis
  • ​Eating high levels of healthy fats and vegetables

Instead of releasing insulin after eating, ketosis will change your body’s metabolism to allow it to release ketones!

These are the chemicals your liver produces that allow you to burn fat for energy instead of sugar.

So, by correctly implementing a keto diet with intermittent fasting, you should have no problem turning your health around.

Keto and IF are perfect for anyone who:

  • Struggles to lose weight
  • Has chronic high blood sugar
  • Is Type 2 Diabetic
  • Has constant heartburn or acid reflux/GERD
  • Has a severe lack of energy
